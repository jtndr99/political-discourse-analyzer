Cross-Framework Analysis Radar
Vilfredo Pareto's Lens: Explores the non-rational instincts (Residues) driving the speech and the rhetorical facades (Derivations) used to justify them. Look for Class I (Combinations/persuasion) vs Class II (Group loyalty/tradition/force) residues, and derivations appealing to authority, assertion, sentiments, or verbal sophistry.
Thomas Sowell's Lens: Evaluates whether the discourse assumes a Constrained (Tragic) Vision of human nature (humanity is limited; we must make trade-offs using systemic rules and traditions) or an Unconstrained (Utopian) Vision (humanity is perfectible; problems can be solved directly by reason and expert planners).
Gustave Le Bon & Eric Hoffer's Lens: Highlights crowd suggestibility, contagion, absolute affirmation, and repetition (Le Bon). Maps the True Believer psychology—frustration, self-renunciation, escape into a holy collective, and the manufacture of a unifying common devil or enemy (Hoffer).
Michel Foucault's Lens: Analyzes the linkages of Power and Knowledge (pouvoir-savoir). Investigates the Regimes of Truth constructed to make certain truth claims function as authoritative, and checks for biopolitical regulation (governing population metrics) or disciplinary/surveillance normalization mechanisms.
